01 / About

I'm happiest when a messy dataset starts making sense — and even happier when someone who isn't a data person can understand it too.

My path into computer science started with curiosity about how systems behave — why an app feels smooth, why a chart makes a number click instantly when a spreadsheet never could. That curiosity turned into a habit: I pick things apart, in code and in data, until I understand how they actually work.

I'm drawn to data science and visualization because they sit right at that intersection — part logic, part storytelling. Give me a pile of numbers and I'll look for the shape hiding inside it: a trend in a line plot, an outlier in a scatter, a distribution in a box plot that tells you more than the average ever could.

I'm just as comfortable on the software side. Building GramaConnect, a village issue-reporting system, taught me that good engineering isn't only about clean code — it's about making sure the people who need a system most can actually use it. That's the kind of work I want to keep doing: practical, useful, and a little bit human.

Outside of formal projects, I'm the kind of learner who finishes one certification and immediately opens the next tab. Quick to pick things up, particular about getting the details right, and always looking for the next problem worth solving.
